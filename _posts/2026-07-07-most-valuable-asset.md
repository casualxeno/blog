What makes a car good? Speed, reliability and comfortability. A book should be understable, not too small or not too large, have concise and clear ideas, and be well-written.

What makes a person good? If someone has a perfect luxury item, we don't say "This person is good because of this item". Instead, we say: "This person has a good item". The item belongs to its owner, but the owner is not defined solely by the item. While we can infer things about a person indirectly by looking at their clothes, color palette, interests, these things will reveal only information about their beliefs, tastes, not their overall character.

One's only valuable asset is their virtues. It doesn't matter if a person lives in poverty or luxury, has higher education or not - they are only defined by their inner thoughts and opinions, which can be expressed via different means.

The virtues are: honesty, reliability, acting neither too soft nor too hard, the ability to converge to [a golden mean][0], learning from mistakes and progressing through life.

If a bag has a hole, it loses its purpose and is discarded. Similarly, if a person loses their moral compass, their virtues become questionable. Meanwhile some individuals may act on undesirable events, believing they can create more positive value from the negative impact.

Consider thieves or corrupted politicians who might steal money, conviced that they will do more good stuff than the original owner. This idea is visible in [Dostoevskiy's book Crime and Punishment][1].

Farewell
