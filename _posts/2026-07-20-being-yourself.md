"AI is good!", "AI is bad!", "You should learn Vim!", "You should learn this!", "You should learn that!", and more and more different opinions... In the end, a passive listener, lacking structure and meaning, doesn't know what is right and wrong, and eventually becomes a collection of ideas without purpose. While this isn't inherently wrong, this person might start recommending useless information. Therefore, it's better to experiment and form your own opinions and foundations rather than constantly listening to everyone.

The following dialogue is an example of talking to a passive listener:

\- I'm intelligent because I've read a lot of different opinions and watched numerous videos.

\- Well, demonstrate your intelligence by showcasing your accomplishments, any projects you've finished, or sharing your opinions or thoughts.

\- *Someone* said something, and *someone* else did something else.

\- While you're a great reader and watcher, I'm curious to know **what you've done yourself**.

\- I know what *someone* else is doing or thinking and they are better than me at it.

\- If I want to understand *someone's* thoughts, I'll do that myself. I'm interested in your ideas and what you've achieved.
