Many real objects have physical properties that are determined by the natural law, but then we take them and assign symbolic value. Sometimes the symbolic applications become so strong they completely overshadow physical properties. For instance, a banknote is simply a peace of paper, yet society collectively agrees that it represents purchasing power. As a result, we stop focusing on its physical form and instead recognize its symbolic value.

Babies and young children don't yet understand these shared social beliefs, because they haven't developed a sense that adults acquire over time. In the same, way we create imagined value not only for objects but also for our past and future. Unlike animals which typically respond only to current situations, humans often worry about both the past and the future.

It's safe to say, we should be more cautious about assigning too much importance to imagined problems that may never become reality. Instead, we should live in the present, and do our best. Many outcomes depend on other people on different events beyond our control. Although succes is never guaranteed, we can always take the next step toward our goals.
