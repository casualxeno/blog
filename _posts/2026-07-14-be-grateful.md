Every non-natural thing we encounter today - appartments, bridges, mobile phones, computers, the internet, books, literature, arts - was built by countless people in the past. These developers, scientists, and others can no longer use these advancements. Similarly, in the future, our descedants will likely use even more advanced technologies and new discoveries that we will no longer be able to utilize.

We should be grateful for what our ancestors gave us. Let's not forget the countless hours they put to their work. Consider the technologies, lights, water systems, paper, plastic, oil processing, ore mining, chemistry and everything else we use daily. Yet, we may not even realize that some people in the past would have given their entire net worth just to use the very things we use every day.

At the same time, it doesn't mean we should always be thinking about this. Instead, we should find a golden mean and use the things we have normally, but also be grateful for them. This will help us train ourselves to be thankful and enjoy life even during difficult times.
